# Brotherhood of Steel

> [!NOTE] Typo Need to Correct and Check-in 
> Brotherhood of Steal->Steel

**Founded:** 2077 (post-[[The Great War]])  
**Type:** Quasi-religious military order  
**Goal:** Preserve and control technology  
**Symbol:** Winged sword and gears

## Overview
The Brotherhood of Steel is one of the most recognizable factions in the [[Fallout Universe]]. Founded by U.S. Army soldiers who survived the nuclear war, they are dedicated to preserving pre-war technology and preventing its misuse - though their methods vary by chapter.

## Origins
- Founded by Roger Maxson
- U.S. Army deserters at Mariposa Military Base
- Witnessed horrific FEV experiments pre-war
- Survived [[The Great War]] in bunker
- Named after bonds formed between soldiers

## Core Beliefs
- Technology must be preserved
- Wasteland dwellers can't be trusted with advanced tech
- Power Armor and energy weapons their specialty
- Scribes research, Knights fight, Paladins lead
- Isolationist tendencies

## Hierarchy
- **Elder:** Overall leader of chapter
- **Paladins:** Elite warriors
- **Knights:** Soldiers and combat personnel
- **Scribes:** Researchers and technical experts
- **Initiates:** Recruits and trainees

## In Games

### [[Fallout 3]] (Capital Wasteland)
- Elder Lyons leads
- Helps protect wastelanders
- More altruistic than other chapters
- Alliance with player possible
- Conflicts with [[Enclave]]

### [[Fallout 4]] (Commonwealth)
- Elder Maxson leads
- More traditionalist approach
- Arrives in airship (Prydwen)
- Opposes [[The Institute]]
- Destroy synths as "abominations"

### [[Fallout New Vegas]] (Mojave)
- Isolated bunker chapter
- Led by Elder McNamara
- Losing war with [[New California Republic]]
- Can ally with or oppose player

### [[Fallout 76]] (Appalachia)
- First Fallout game chronologically to show them
- Led by Paladin Rahmani
- Internal conflicts over mission

## In [[Fallout TV Series]]
- Major faction in show
- [[Maximus]] is Brotherhood squire
- Knight Titus is corrupted member
- Pursuing cold fusion technology
- Represent military might and ideology

## Technology
- **Power Armor:** Signature equipment
- **Energy Weapons:** Laser and plasma rifles
- **Vertibirds:** Military aircraft (some chapters)
- **Liberty Prime:** Giant robot (East Coast)

## Philosophy
- "Steel be with you" greeting
- Technology preservation at any cost
- Distrust of outsiders
- Military discipline and honor
- Belief in their superiority

## Conflicts
- Often clash with [[Enclave]]
- War with [[New California Republic]]
- Oppose [[The Institute]]
- Destroy synths and mutants
- Competition for tech resources

## Criticism
- Hoard technology instead of sharing
- Elitist and xenophobic
- Slow to help common wastelanders
- Internal power struggles
- Varying interpretations of mission
