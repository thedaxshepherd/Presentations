# Fallout 4

**Release Date:** November 10, 2015  
**Developer:** Bethesda Game Studios  
**Setting:** The Commonwealth (Boston area)  
**Protagonist:** Sole Survivor

## Overview
Fallout 4 is Bethesda's second mainline Fallout game, set in post-apocalyptic Boston. The game follows the Sole Survivor, a pre-war character cryogenically frozen in [[Vault 111]], who awakens 210 years later to search for their kidnapped son.

## Setting
- **The Commonwealth:** Boston and surrounding area
- 210 years after [[The Great War]]
- Notable locations: Diamond City, Goodneighbor, Bunker Hill
- Heavy synth presence
- [[The Institute]] beneath MIT

## Main Story
1. Pre-war life in Sanctuary Hills
2. Nuclear war, enter [[Vault 111]]
3. Cryogenically frozen
4. Witness spouse's murder, son Shaun kidnapped
5. Awaken 210 years later
6. Search for Shaun across Commonwealth
7. Discover Shaun is "Father," leader of [[The Institute]]
8. Choose faction to support

## Major Factions
- **[[The Institute]]** - Underground scientists, main antagonists
- **[[Brotherhood of Steel]]** - Led by Elder Maxson, anti-synth
- **Railroad** - Free synths from Institute
- **Minutemen** - Commonwealth defense militia

## Gameplay Innovations
- Settlement building system
- Crafting and modification
- Power Armor as vehicle
- Voiced protagonist
- Companion romance options
- Improved graphics and combat

## Key Characters
- **Sole Survivor** - Player (Nate or Nora)
- **Shaun/Father** - Son, becomes Institute director
- **Preston Garvey** - Minutemen leader
- **Paladin Danse** - [[Brotherhood of Steel]] member
- **Nick Valentine** - Synth detective
- **Piper Wright** - Journalist companion
- **Kellogg** - Mercenary who killed spouse

## Notable Locations
- [[Vault 111]] - Starting location
- Diamond City - Fenway Park settlement
- Goodneighbor - Criminal haven
- The Glowing Sea - Ground zero
- Institute - Underground facility

## Settlement System
- Build and manage settlements
- Attract settlers
- Defend from raids
- Supply lines between settlements
- Power, water, food, defense

## DLC Expansions
1. **Automatron** - Robot companions
2. **Wasteland Workshop** - Settlement additions
3. **Far Harbor** - Island mystery
4. **Contraptions Workshop** - Manufacturing
5. **Vault-Tec Workshop** - Build your own Vault
6. **Nuka-World** - Raider theme park

## Synth Controversy
- Gen-3 synths indistinguishable from humans
- Debate over synth rights
- Institute creates and enslaves them
- Railroad fights for freedom
- [[Brotherhood of Steel]] wants them destroyed

## Four Faction Endings
1. Institute - Lead as new director
2. Brotherhood - Destroy Institute
3. Railroad - Free synths, destroy Institute
4. Minutemen - Destroy Institute, spare other factions

## Themes
- What defines humanity?
- Parental love and loss
- Synthetic vs biological life
- Rebuilding society
- Pre-war nostalgia
