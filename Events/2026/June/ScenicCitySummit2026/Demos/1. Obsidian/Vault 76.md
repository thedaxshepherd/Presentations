# Vault 76

**Location:** Appalachia (West Virginia)  
**Game:** [[Fallout 76]]  
**Type:** Control Vault (functional)  
**Reclamation Day:** 2102 (25 years after [[The Great War]])

## Overview
Vault 76 is one of the few [[Vault-Tec]] control Vaults - designed to actually work as intended. Built to house America's "best and brightest," it was always meant to open 25 years after the war to help rebuild.

## The Experiment
**No experiment** - This was a legitimate survival Vault meant to:
- House cream of American society
- Preserve knowledge and skills
- Reopen on schedule
- Reclaim and rebuild America

## Reclamation Day
- October 27, 2102
- 25 years after sealing
- All residents exit Vault
- Mission: Rebuild Appalachia
- Players are residents leaving Vault

## Notable Features
- Celebratory Reclamation Day party
- Well-stocked and maintained
- Residents trained for surface survival
- Historical exhibits and memorabilia
- Overseer provides guidance

## Vault Residents
- All player characters in [[Fallout 76]]
- "Best and brightest" of pre-war America
- Scientists, engineers, doctors
- Selected for rebuilding skills
- Online multiplayer represents different residents

## The Overseer
- Leaves Vault early
- Sets up holotape messages
- Guides residents' journey
- Explores Appalachia ahead
- Mysterious absence

## Gameplay Role
- Tutorial area for [[Fallout 76]]
- Players exit on Reclamation Day
- Cannot re-enter after leaving
- Symbol of hope and new beginning
- Contrast to failed experiment Vaults

## Appalachia
- Relatively untouched by nuclear war
- Lush and green (compared to other wasteland)
- New threats (Scorchbeasts)
- Empty towns and settlements
- Players must rebuild society

## Themes
- Optimism and hope
- Rebuilding civilization
- Cooperation (multiplayer)
- Legacy of pre-war world
- Second chances
