# Ghouls

**Type:** Mutated humans  
**Cause:** Extreme radiation exposure  
**Lifespan:** Potentially immortal  
**Variants:** Standard, Feral, Glowing

## Overview
Ghouls are humans who survived massive radiation exposure during or after [[The Great War]], resulting in severe physical mutations. Their skin becomes necrotic and they lose all hair, but they gain immunity to radiation and extreme longevity. Ghouls are one of [[The Fallout Universe]]'s most tragic and morally complex creatures.

## Physical Characteristics

### Appearance
- Necrotic, decaying skin
- No hair (completely bald)
- Exposed tissue and muscle
- Sunken features
- Raspy voice
- Appear zombie-like
- Smell of decay

### Mutations
- Radiation immunity
- Radiation actually heals them
- Extended lifespan (centuries)
- Don't age normally
- Slower wound healing
- Constant pain (varies)
- Physical degradation

## Types of Ghouls

### Standard Ghouls (Sentient)
- Retain full intelligence
- Memories intact
- Personality unchanged
- Can communicate normally
- Integrated into society (sometimes)
- "Smoothskin" for non-ghouls

### Feral Ghouls
- Lost mental faculties
- Zombie-like behavior
- Aggressive and hostile
- No language ability
- Animalistic
- What all ghouls fear becoming

### Glowing Ones
- Heavily irradiated variant
- Emit radiation
- Glow green
- Heal other ghouls
- Highly dangerous
- Rare and powerful

## How Ghouls Are Created

### Radiation Exposure
- Massive doses of radiation
- Not enough to kill immediately
- Changes cellular structure
- Preserves consciousness (initially)
- Gradual transformation
- Extremely painful process

### Timing
- Many created during [[The Great War]]
- Nuclear blasts directly
- Fallout exposure
- Also created post-war
- Ongoing process
- Radiation still creates ghouls

### Not Everyone Becomes One
- Unknown what determines survival
- Most die from radiation
- Some become ghouls
- Extremely rare occurrence
- Genetic factors possibly
- Luck/curse
