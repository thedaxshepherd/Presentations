# The Great War

**Date:** October 23, 2077  
**Duration:** Approximately 2 hours  
**Result:** End of civilization

## Overview
The Great War was the final nuclear exchange between the United States and China that devastated Earth in the [[Fallout Universe]]. The war lasted just two hours but resulted in the near-total destruction of human civilization and the creation of the radioactive wasteland.

## Causes

### Resource Wars
- Global petroleum depletion
- European Commonwealth collapses
- Middle Eastern oil fields run dry
- Resource conflicts escalate
- US annexes Canada

### US-China Conflict
- Competition for remaining resources
- Chinese invasion of Alaska
- American liberation of Anchorage
- Escalating tensions
- Nuclear threats

## The Attack

### October 23, 2077
- **09:42 EST:** Air raid sirens across America
- **09:47 EST:** Nuclear launches confirmed
- **10:00 EST:** [[Vault-Tec]] Vaults seal
- **Next 2 hours:** Global nuclear exchange
- Billions die instantly or in aftermath

### Targets
- Major American cities destroyed
- Military installations hit
- Chinese mainland devastated
- Global nuclear winter begins
- Surface becomes uninhabitable

## Immediate Aftermath

### Survivors
- Those in [[Vault-Tec]] Vaults
- Deep underground bunkers
- Remote or lucky individuals
- Ghouls (radiation-preserved humans)
- Military remnants ([[Enclave]])

### Environmental Impact
- Massive radiation levels
- Nuclear winter
- Ecosystem collapse
- Water contamination
- Sky turns dark

## Long-Term Effects

### 200+ Years Later
- Radiation still prevalent
- Mutated creatures (Radroaches, Deathclaws)
- Permanent environmental damage
- Scarce resources
- Scattered human settlements

### Societal Breakdown
- Pre-war government gone
- New factions emerge
- [[Brotherhood of Steel]]
- [[New California Republic]]
- Raider groups

## The Vaults

### [[Vault-Tec]]'s Role
- 122 Vaults sealed during war
- Saved select population
- Experiments began immediately
- [[Vault 101]], [[Vault 111]], [[Vault 33]], etc.
- Some residents emerge decades later

## Cultural Memory

### In Games
- Every Fallout game starts here
- Pre-war world shown in flashbacks
- Ruins of old world explored
- "War Never Changes" tagline

### In [[Fallout TV Series]]
- Pre-war life depicted
- Nuclear strike shown
- Vault sealing dramatized
- Aftermath explored
