# Maximus

**Played by:** Aaron Moten  
**Faction:** [[Brotherhood of Steel]]  
**Rank:** Squire, later Knight  
**Role:** Protagonist of [[Fallout TV Series]]

## Background
Maximus was orphaned as a child when his town was destroyed by raiders. Rescued and raised by the [[Brotherhood of Steel]], he was indoctrinated into their philosophy of technological preservation and trained as a squire.

## Character Traits
- **Conflicted:** Questions Brotherhood methods
- **Loyal:** Despite doubts, committed to mission
- **Ambitious:** Seeks to prove himself
- **Compassionate:** More empathetic than Brotherhood norm
- **Survivor:** Adapted from trauma

## Story Arc
1. Serving as squire to Knight Titus
2. Knight Titus killed, Maximus takes Power Armor
3. Impersonates knight to gain respect
4. Sent on mission to retrieve target
5. Meets [[Lucy MacLean]]
6. Torn between Brotherhood and personal morality
7. Questions faction's extremism

## Brotherhood Training
- Raised with military discipline
- Taught technology is sacred
- Power Armor operation
- Combat training
- Brotherhood ideology and dogma

## Equipment
- T-60 Power Armor (taken from Knight Titus)
- Laser rifle
- Brotherhood of Steel insignia
- Later questioned authenticity

## Relationships

### [[Lucy MacLean]]
- Meets in wasteland
- Unlikely partnership
- Romantic tension
- Cultural clash (Brotherhood vs Vault)
- Teaches her wasteland survival

### Knight Titus
- Abusive superior
- Killed in wasteland

- Complicated feelings about death

### Brotherhood Command
- Struggles with orders
- Questions ruthless methods
- Wants approval but maintains conscience
- Internal conflict about loyalty

## Internal Conflict
- Brotherhood raised him but methods questionable
- Wants to be hero but organization uses violence
- Imposter syndrome (not really a Knight)
- Balancing personal morality vs faction loyalty
- Compassion vs Brotherhood dogma

## Character Development
- Starts as loyal follower
- Begins questioning orders
- Makes independent moral choices
- Torn between duty and conscience
- May leave Brotherhood eventually

## Themes
- Blind loyalty vs independent thought
- Child soldiers and indoctrination
- Finding identity beyond faction
- Moral complexity
- Redemption and choice

## Contrast to Brotherhood
- More compassionate than typical member
- Questions "destroy all abominations" policy
- Sees wasteland dwellers as people
- Willing to help outsiders
- Represents potential reform
