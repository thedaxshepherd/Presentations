# Fallout Universe

**Created by:** Interplay Entertainment (1997)  
**Current Owner:** Bethesda Game Studios  
**Setting:** Post-apocalyptic retrofuturistic America

## Overview
The Fallout universe is a sprawling post-nuclear apocalypse setting spanning video games, a television series, and other media. Set in an alternate timeline where 1950s American culture continued into the future, the world was devastated by [[The Great War]] in 2077, leading to the creation of underground [[Vault-Tec]] shelters and the subsequent exploration of the wasteland.

## Timeline

### Pre-War Era (Before 2077)
- Divergence from our timeline post-WWII
- 1950s culture and aesthetic persist
- Nuclear technology advances rapidly
- Resource wars escalate
- [[Vault-Tec]] begins Vault construction
- Tension between US and China

### [[The Great War]] (October 23, 2077)
- Nuclear exchange destroys civilization
- 2 hours of nuclear bombardment
- Most of humanity killed
- [[Vault-Tec]] Vaults seal
- Surface becomes radioactive wasteland

### Post-War Era (2077-Present)
- Survivors emerge from Vaults
- New societies form in wasteland
- Factions vie for control
- Mutated creatures emerge
- Technology scavenged and rebuilt

## Major Factions

### Pre-War Organizations
- [[Vault-Tec]] - Vault construction company
- [[Enclave]] - Remnants of US government

### Post-War Factions
- [[Brotherhood of Steel]] - Technological preservationists
- [[New California Republic]] (NCR) - Democratic federation
- [[Caesar's Legion]] - Totalitarian slavers
- [[The Institute]] - Underground scientists

## The Vaults

### [[Vault-Tec]] Experiment
- 122 Vaults built across America
- Most were social experiments
- Only a few "control" Vaults
- Each Vault had unique experiment

### Notable Vaults
- [[Vault 101]] - Isolated indefinitely (Fallout 3)
- [[Vault 111]] - Cryogenic preservation (Fallout 4)
- [[Vault 33]] - Utopian society (TV Series)
- [[Vault 13]] - Extended isolation (Fallout 1)
- [[Vault 76]] - Reclamation Day (Fallout 76)

## Video Games

## Core Themes

### Retrofuturism
- 1950s aesthetic in future technology
- Atomic age optimism meets nuclear horror
- Pulp science fiction influences

### War Never Changes
- Recurring series tagline
- Human nature remains constant
- History repeats in wasteland

### Vault Experiments
- [[Vault-Tec]]'s unethical research
- Social and psychological experiments
- Corporate greed and government conspiracy
